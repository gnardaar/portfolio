Flying Lessons

There is a lesson said by one of my favorite characters in all of the film and that/'s, "There is no try...Only do, or do not."-Yoda. Following this principle, I was able to learn probably one of the hardest lifestyles in the world, skateboarding. The massive skill in the sport and the programming industry has to be one of the closest things that I could compare skateboarding with programming. Programming in a sense is so much like skateboarding. With skating at my best, I can achieve great style and ease, but with that comes consequences and dire cautions. At any moment I must think of the best outcome at all times to avoid injuries. Believing the impossible is the most crucial aspect of skateboarding, along with close attention to detail. Now, most people have seen wins and losses in skateboarding. Frustrations come with the territory, and it may seem all hope is gone once you fail. But, what I have learned through the family which is in the skateboarding community, gives me that extra push to persevere and move forward. I could not count how many times I have picked myself up bloody and dirty. What I do know is that I did it every time without exemption! If it wasn't for skating then I would never find myself striving to achieve the impossible every day. I take a unique perspective on life that allows me to free my mind of fear and doubt, and to see what needs to happen. I guess my attitude to programming is a lot like my personal take on skating: I am a tenacious individual! I know that the world will never know what struggles I had to deal with, but in the end, I conquered them individually. Find the beauty in the practice, and most importantly... Never! Under any circumstances give up!
